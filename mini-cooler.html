Overview

For our 3LP subject, we developed an Arduino-based mini-cooler. This device uses an Arduino microcontroller to control the motor speed and an LCD screen to display the current mode. To cool the air, users can place an ice pack in a designated compartment at the back of the device.

Problem

Our teacher challenged us to design a small, portable, and affordable cooler that could be used anywhere. The goal was to create a cooling solution that was both easy to build and cost-effective and also energy efficient

Solution

To achieve a portable and affordable solution, we opted for a 3D-printed casing designed using Fusion 360. This approach made the device easy to build and customize. An Arduino Uno microcontroller was chosen for its beginner-friendliness, controlling the device's operations. An LCD screen was integrated to provide clear visual feedback on the current mode, enhancing user experience. To make it more sustainable, we added a solar panel for free charging.
